I was terrified of student debt.
So I engineered a way out.

As a freshman in high school, I knew college was going to be my path. But there was one thing I was terrified of: student debt. The average private university student borrows $35,000+ after they graduate — and those numbers keep climbing.

So I asked a different question. Not "which college should I go to?" — but "how do I get this credential as fast, as cheaply, and as strategically as possible?"

While registering for sophomore classes, I found out you could earn college credits in high school through AP exams. Then I fell down the rabbit hole: CLEP exams, DSST exams, self-study options — and PSEO, where the state of Minnesota literally pays for you to take college courses while you're still in high school. For free.

I stacked every one of these. I used a tool called Transferology to make sure every single credit would transfer. I made a spreadsheet — one column with my degree requirements, one column with the exam or course that could satisfy each one. I double-checked, triple-checked, quadruple-checked everything.

My graduating class was supposed to be 2023. I finished in 2020. I entered my freshman year as a college senior. I graduated with my BBA in Organizational Management from the University of Minnesota system in ONE YEAR. I was 19. Then I graduated Harvard with my Master's at 21. Bought my first house at 20. Traveled to 18+ countries by 23.

Nobody handed me this. I had to fight my school counselor for PSEO enrollment. I had to personally request AP exams my school didn't even offer. I studied for most exams in 2–3 weeks each. I had to figure out every piece of this puzzle myself — with wrong information pointing me in circles.

That ends with you. This guide is the map I didn't have.
