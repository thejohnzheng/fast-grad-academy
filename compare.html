Decision guide

Which should you take first?

Use the first condition that fits you. The right answer is the one your school will actually accept for your degree.

1

Are you currently in high school?

Consider AP first if your school already offers the course and exam. You get teacher support, a built-in schedule, and a familiar May testing window.

2

Are you an adult or already enrolled in college?

Start with CLEP. It has broad acceptance, year-round scheduling, and a low exam fee compared with paying per credit.

3

Are you active duty, veteran, or military-connected?

Check DSST and CLEP funding. DSST says DANTES can fund first attempts for eligible military students, and College Board lists CLEP military benefits.

4

Want the fastest possible path?

CLEP usually wins because you do not have to wait for May. Verify the exam first, study for the closest requirement, then schedule the test.

FAQ

Questions students ask before choosing.

Can I use CLEP credits to graduate early?

Yes, if your college accepts the specific CLEP exam for your degree requirements. College Board says CLEP can help students earn three or more credits per passing exam at participating institutions, but you should verify your school's policy before registering.

Do all colleges accept CLEP credits?

No. College Board lists CLEP as accepted at over 3,000 U.S. colleges and universities, but every institution decides which exams count, what score is required, and whether the credit applies to your major, gen eds, or electives.

Is CLEP harder than AP?

It depends on the subject and your background. AP is usually a full-year class with a May exam. CLEP is usually self-study, mostly multiple choice, and easier to schedule quickly if you already know the material.

Can I take CLEP exams while enrolled in college?

Often yes, but schools can block duplicate credit, cap transfer credit, or require that some credits be taken in residence. Ask your advisor or registrar for written confirmation before paying for the exam.

How many CLEP exams can I take?

College Board offers 34 CLEP exams. Your real limit is your school policy: residency rules, major requirements, transfer-credit caps, and whether the same subject duplicates a course you already took.

What's the difference between CLEP and DSST?

Both can turn exam scores into college credit. CLEP is administered by College Board and has broader published acceptance. DSST is run through Prometric/Get College Credit, has 30+ exams, and is especially relevant for eligible military learners.

Are CLEP exams multiple choice?

Most are multiple choice. College Board notes that College Composition and Spanish with Writing include essays that are combined with the multiple-choice score.

How do I find out if my school accepts CLEP?

Start with the CLEP college credit policy search, then confirm the exact exam, required score, and degree requirement with your advisor or registrar in writing.
