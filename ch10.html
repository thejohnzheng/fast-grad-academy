Chapter 10

Your Personalized Roadmap

The right strategy depends on where you are right now. Find your starting point below and follow the roadmap designed for your situation.

🌱
9th — 10th Grade
You have the most time and flexibility. Start with 2-3 easy CLEP exams (College Composition, American Government, Introductory Psychology). Take 1 AP exam by self-study. Begin conversations about PSEO for junior year. Goal: 15-20 credits by end of sophomore year.

Summer strategy: Take one exam per month (Jun-Aug) = $12,000+ saved in one summer.
Maximum Potential
🔥
11th — 12th Grade
Prime time for acceleration. Enroll in PSEO immediately. Stack CLEP exams during breaks. Take 3-4 AP exams (self-study the ones your school doesn't offer). Use DSST to fill gaps. Goal: 45-60+ credits by graduation.

Critical: Use Transferology NOW to verify every credit will transfer to your target schools.
Peak Acceleration
🎓
College Freshman / Sophomore
Not too late. Use CLEP and DSST to knock out remaining gen-ed requirements instead of paying full tuition for them. Focus on exams that directly replace courses in your degree plan. This can shave 1-2 semesters off your timeline.

Key move: Talk to your academic advisor about which CLEP/DSST exams your school accepts and the maximum transfer credits allowed.
Still Powerful
💼
Working Adults
Your life experience is an asset. CLEP exams test knowledge you may already have from work. Personal Finance, Intro to Business, and Management are common wins. Prior Learning Assessment can convert work experience into credits. Consider WGU (Western Governors University) for competency-based progression.
Experience = Credits
🎖️
Military & Veterans
You have the best deal in education. Here's exactly what you get:

• Free CLEP & DSST exams — DANTES (Defense Activity for Non-Traditional Education Support) covers 100% of exam fees for active-duty servicemembers. Take them at on-base education centers at zero cost.
• Joint Services Transcript (JST) — your military training converts directly to college credit. Request yours at jst.doded.mil.
• Tuition Assistance (TA) — up to $250/credit hour while active duty. Stack this with free CLEP credits for a completely free degree.
• GI Bill — covers tuition, housing, and books. Use it AFTER exhausting free CLEP/DSST and TA so you don't waste GI Bill on courses you could have tested out of.
• Best path: Max out free CLEP/DSST → use TA for remaining courses → save GI Bill for grad school or transfer to dependents.
Special Benefits
🌏
International Students
The system works globally. CLEP exams can be taken at international test centers. Many U.S. universities accept CLEP credits from international students. Foreign language CLEP exams (Spanish, French, German) can be easier if you're already bilingual or multilingual.
Global Access

The Five University Paths for Maximum Acceleration

Once you've pre-loaded credits through CLEP, Sophia, and Study.com, you need a university that accepts massive transfer loads and lets you move at your own pace. The accelerated-degree community relies on five schools — each with a different sweet spot:

🏛️
WGU (Western Governors University)
Competency-based. Flat-rate per 6-month term. Pass exams (OAs) and projects (PAs) to advance — no seat time required. Pre-assess to skip material you already know. Accepts up to 90 transfer credits (75% of degree). Pass/fail grading means a 3.0 GPA cap, but graduates are accepted at Georgia Tech, Penn State, and more for grad school.

Best for: Students who can self-study and test out of courses rapidly. IT, Business, Education, and Healthcare degrees.
Self-Paced King
🎓
UMPI YourPace (Univ. of Maine at Presque Isle)
Flat-rate per 8-week term. Letter grades (enables 4.0 GPA). Project and research-based assessments instead of proctored exams. Accepts up to 90 transfer credits. Liberal Studies degree = 45 credits in major, up to 75 free electives — maximum flexibility for transfers.

Best for: Students targeting grad school (letter grades matter), or those who prefer writing/projects over exams.
GPA Maximizer
🏛️
Thomas Edison State University (TESU)
The gold standard for alternative credit. Accepts up to 120 transfer credits from CLEP, DSST, Sophia, Study.com, StraighterLine, UExcel, and more. Has its own exam program (TECEP) covering subjects other exams don't. Liberal Studies degree is the #1 recommended pathway in the credit-by-exam community. Nearly every sub-$10K degree story uses TESU.

Best for: Students maximizing alternative credits. The most flexible transfer policy in the country. Based in New Jersey, fully online.
tesu.edu →
Community Favorite
🏛️
Excelsior University
No residency requirement. 100% exam-based degree possible. Accepts massive transfer credit from all major sources. Has its own exam program (UExcel) with subjects CLEP doesn't cover. Josh Kaufman's famous "$4,000 bachelor's degree" pathway runs through Excelsior. Liberal Arts degree accepts nearly all alternative credit types.

Best for: Students who want to complete an entire degree through exams and online courses. No classes required. Based in New York, fully online.
excelsior.edu →
The Exam-Only Path
🏛️
Charter Oak State College
Connecticut's public online college designed for alternative credit. Accepts CLEP, DSST, ACE credits, portfolio assessment, and military credit. General Studies degree is built for maximum transfer flexibility. Lower tuition than many alternatives. Accredited by NECHE (same as Yale, MIT).

Best for: Adult learners, military, or anyone with a mix of non-traditional credits. Portfolio assessment can convert work experience into credits.
