Breaking the Education System

Most families don't realize it, but the college system has built-in pathways that let you earn a full degree in a fraction of the time and cost. These aren't loopholes or shortcuts — they're official, accredited programs that universities themselves accept. The problem is almost nobody talks about them.

I graduated college in one year. Not because I'm a genius. Not because I took 17 classes a semester (just kidding). Because I found these pathways — CLEP, DSST, AP, PSEO, dual enrollment — and used them strategically.

"Hope is not a strategy. Too often, I hear people say 'Hopefully, it will transfer.' Do. Not. Follow. This. Mindset."

These mechanisms allow you to earn college credits at a fraction of the cost and time. Most students don't know they exist. The ones who do save tens of thousands of dollars and graduate years ahead of their peers — with the same regionally accredited degrees.

The Numbers Don't Lie

Average cost of a college credit at a public university: $350–600 (NCES, 2024). Cost of a CLEP exam: $97. Each CLEP exam can earn you 3–6 credits. That's potentially $2,100+ in credit value for $97. And with Modern States, the exam fee is reimbursed — making it completely free.

A single summer of CLEP exams (taking one per month from June through August) could save you over $12,000 in tuition. An entire high school career of strategic credit stacking? That's how you get to $85,000+ saved, like I did.

Real Students, Real Results

This isn't just my story. Students across the country are using credit-by-exam to transform their college trajectory:

  • Students who earn 12+ CLEP credits save an average of 9–14 months toward degree completion (College Board)
  • One student earned 52 college credits through CLEP and DSST exams — graduating early and saving thousands without ever sitting in a traditional classroom (Peterson's)
  • An adult learner saved over $10,000 on her degree using CLEP through Modern States' free prep program (Modern States)
  • Passing a CLEP exam correlates with a 5.5% increase in degree completion rates — students who use these tools are more likely to finish, not less (College Board)
  • The Senior VP of Human Relations at HCA Healthcare has said graduates from competency-based programs arrive with the skills employers need and are ready to rise to any challenge (WGU employer testimonials)
